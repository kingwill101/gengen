Society and Dancehall

Jamaicans have a tendency of shifting the goal post of where blame should be attributed. One of, if not the most popular, is the argument that dancehall music is the reflection of the Jamaican society rather than the reflector that blinds the eyes of the easily influenced. This argument in my opinion is very shallow. Yes, in the earlier days that may have been how it started. The political climate in the 70s and 80s, significantly contributed to crime and violence. Many area leaders came into being controlling/leading different communities and factions. An era well documented by political experts way more qualified than I am. Not being able to do much, musicians back then started singing about what happened.

An interesting era in Jamaica's history , circa 2004-2010. The period i went through high school. High school is where my understanding of Dancehall culture truly started. Admittedly i grew up in an environment where i only ever heard my father playing reggae music that originated in the early 60's up until the late 90's, and of course the obligated healthy dose of gospel music that most Jamaicans listened to on a Sunday morning.

Commute for me was home-school-home, home-church-home never truly being able to interact with elements outside of said spheres. That changed on the first day of high school, having to now commute to a different parish I only ever visited once for orientation. Transportation came via a mini bus or the dedicated metro that went directly to school. I now had to experience what society was truly like, if you have ever had to travel anywhere in Jamaica via the same methods, you will definitely understand the meaning behind my words.

Interestingly, within the same period we got the Gaza vs Gully feud between Vybz Kartel and Movado. Day after day traveling to and from school I could look forward to hear the next installment of a response from either side. We got a lot of interesting lyrics, arguably 80% of said lyrics were about committing murders. My fellow classmates pitted themselves against each other, declaring their allegiance to Gully or Gaza. Said feud eventually led to the government having to intercede bringing both sides to Jamaica house.
